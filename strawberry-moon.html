The Strawberry Moon

The lowest, warmest full moon of the year — named for ripening wild strawberries, not its colour. When it is, why it glows amber, and how to catch it at moonrise.

Short answer
The Strawberry Moon is June's full moon. In 2026 it falls early — May 31 at 08:46 UTC (also a calendar blue moon). It's named for the June wild-strawberry harvest in Native American tradition, not its colour — though it often looks amber because it's the lowest full moon of the year, seen through more atmosphere near the horizon.

Why "Strawberry"?

The name comes from the Algonquin peoples of northeastern North America, who used June's full moon to mark the brief window when wild strawberries ripen and are gathered. It's a seasonal harvest marker, like most traditional full-moon names. Other cultures called the same moon the Rose Moon, Honey Moon or Mead Moon — June was traditionally the month of weddings and honey harvest, which is also where the word "honeymoon" may come from.

Why it looks amber and huge

Because June's full moon falls near the summer solstice — when the Sun rides highest — the Moon, sitting opposite the Sun, takes the lowest path of the year. It skims the southern horizon all night. Light from a low Moon passes through far more atmosphere, which scatters away blue wavelengths and leaves a warm amber-to-rose glow. Combine that with the "moon illusion" (our brain perceives a horizon Moon as larger) and the Strawberry Moon often looks like a giant golden lantern just after it rises.

Frequently asked questions

Does the Strawberry Moon actually turn pink?
Not because of its name. But because it's the lowest full moon of the year, its light passes through extra atmosphere and it frequently looks amber, orange or rosy — especially right at moonrise. So it often is warm-coloured, just not for the reason the name suggests.
When is the best time to see it?
At moonrise, around sunset, looking toward the southeast. That's when the low path, the extra atmosphere and the moon illusion combine for the biggest, most golden view.
